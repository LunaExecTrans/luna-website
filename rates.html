How it works

Three doors, one locked total.

No black box. No surge surprise. Here’s the trip your dollar takes from rate card to receipt.

  1. 01

    The owner sets the rate card.

    Inside Luna Dispatch, the owner edits a private console with one row per vehicle — base, per-mile, hourly minimum and the flat rates for MIA, FLL and PBI airports. Surge multipliers are scheduled by date (Art Basel, F1, NYE), and corporate volume tiers unlock discounts at agreed monthly thresholds.

    • Base · what the chauffeur starts at
    • Per mile · trip-distance component
    • Hourly min · floor for as-directed bookings
    • Airport flats · MIA · FLL · PBI per vehicle
  2. 02

    The engine calculates your quote.

    When you fill the booking form, the system picks the right line of the rate card (airport flat if your pickup is MIA, FLL or PBI — otherwise base + per-mile or hourly minimum), then multiplies by the active surge for your date, then subtracts your corporate discount if you’re on a volume tier.

    flat or (base + miles × per‑mile) × surge − corporate volume % = your locked total
  3. 03

    You see one number. Locked.

    The booking modal shows the total — gratuity, fuel, flight tracking and the first 15 minutes of wait time already included. Dispatch confirms the chauffeur and the rate by phone within 15 minutes. Anything beyond the quoted route (extra stops, wait beyond 15 minutes, tolls, parking) is billed at cost on the final receipt.

    • 20% gratuity included
    • Fuel surcharge included
    • Flight tracking included
    • Bottled water & climate control

How you’re charged

A hold today. A charge after.

Card-on-file pre-authorization protects the reservation. Nothing settles until the chauffeur completes the ride.

  1. 1

    Card on file, pre-authorization hold.

    When you reserve, your card is authorized for the estimated total via Stripe — a hold, not a charge. Luna never touches the card number; details go straight from your browser to the Stripe network.

  2. 2

    Dispatch confirms inside 15 minutes.

    A real person at Luna Dispatch reviews the ride, assigns a chauffeur and vehicle, and confirms the final quote by phone or email. If anything in the rate changes — extra stops, a vehicle upgrade — you approve before the hold is touched.

  3. 3

    Captured after the ride is delivered.

    When your chauffeur drops you off, Luna Dispatch captures the exact final amount from the pre-authorization — including any at-cost tolls, parking or extra wait time. The hold releases automatically if anything goes uncaptured. You receive an itemized receipt by email.

Stripe PCI-compliant. Luna never stores or sees your card details. The pre-authorization hold is released within 7 days if a reservation is canceled before pickup.

Understanding the rate card

Every lever, in plain English.

Six dials live inside Luna Dispatch. Here’s what each one does to the number you see at booking.

Base

The per-hour starting rate.

The hourly figure for the vehicle — what the chauffeur’s time costs. It anchors every hourly reservation and is the multiplier behind the minimum and any per-mile add.

e.g. Mercedes S-Class · $95/hr

Per mile

Distance component.

Added on top of the base for long point-to-point routes. Quoted only when the route isn’t covered by an airport flat or hourly booking — otherwise the base + flat already include it.

e.g. $3.50/mi for an Escalade transfer

Hourly minimum

The floor for as-directed.

The smallest reservation Luna can confirm for that vehicle. Protects the chauffeur’s time when a quick errand would otherwise cost less than the dispatch overhead of arranging it.

e.g. S-Class min 2 hr · Sprinter min 4 hr

Airport flats

One number, terminal to door.

Per-vehicle locked rates for the three South Florida airports — MIA, FLL, PBI. Replaces base + mileage so airport transfers stay predictable, with meet-and-greet and flight tracking already included.

e.g. MIA → Brickell Escalade · $135 flat

Surge windows

Scheduled peak multipliers.

A date-range multiplier (typically 1.25× to 1.75×) applied during pre-announced peak weeks — Art Basel, F1, Ultra, NYE, Super Bowl. Always visible before you confirm, never reactive.

e.g. Art Basel · Dec 4 → 7 · 1.50×

Corporate volume

Discount tiers for accounts.

Corporate accounts unlock discount tiers (typically 5%, 8%, 12%) at agreed monthly ride thresholds. Applied automatically once your account passes the threshold — shown on every confirmation.

e.g. 50+ rides/mo · 8% off every ride

All six are edited by the owner inside Luna Dispatch » Pricing. Dispatchers see the rate card read-only. Want a custom corporate quote? Open a corporate account.
